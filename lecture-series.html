About This Series

This lecture blog series is designed for students in tertiary institutions studying Organisational Communication as part of their academic programme. Each topic is presented as a self-contained blog lecture that can be read independently or followed sequentially as a complete course.

Every lecture includes detailed concept explanations with key concept boxes, real-world workplace examples, references to contemporary academic sources (2020 onwards), structured time allocations for reading and study, and examination practice questions with mark allocations to help students prepare for assessments.

The series adopts a mix citation approach — key theories and models are cited with academic references, while explanations and examples are presented in clear, accessible prose suitable for tertiary-level study.

How to Use This Series

Each lecture is self-contained and can be read independently. However, the topics are sequenced to build progressively — starting with foundational concepts (Topics 1–2), advancing through specific communication types and challenges (Topics 3–8), and concluding with integrative and practical topics (Topics 9–10). For best results, follow the time allocations provided in each lecture: read the content, take notes on key concepts, then attempt the examination practice questions.
